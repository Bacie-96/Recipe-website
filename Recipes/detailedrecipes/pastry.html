Butter buns

500g plain flour (4 cups)

250 ml milk

1 tsp salt

7g yeast

1 egg

50g butter

3 tbsp sugar

Instructions

  1. Mix milk, butter, and warm on the stove.
  2. Mix flour, salt, sugar and make a well in the center.
  3. Put yeast and egg in the well, pour in the milk mixture, and mix (the mixture should not be runny).
  4. Sprinkle flour on the surface, knead the dough, and place it in an oiled bowl.
  5. Cover with a wet dish towel and leave it to rise until doubled in size.
  6. Knead again to knock the air out of the dough, cut small pieces, and roll them into balls.
  7. Place the balls on a baking tray and cover with a wet cloth.
  8. Let them rise again. When doubled, glaze with (2 tbsp of sugar & 1 tbsp of water microwaved for 2 seconds) and bake.

Butter Cookies

Ingredients

225g butter, softened

140g sugar

2 egg yolks

1½ tsp vanilla extract

2 cups all-purpose flour

4 tsp milk

1 tbsp vanilla essence

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk cream together butter, sugar, and salt until combined.
  2. Mix in egg yolks and vanilla.
  3. Add flour and mix until crumbly, then add 2 tsp milk. If needed, add another 1–2 tsp milk for a more pipeable consistency.
  4. Roll out the dough and cut cookie shapes / transfer to a piping bag with a star tip.
  5. Pipe dough into rounds onto ungreased baking sheets.
  6. Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown on the bottom.
  7. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Scones

Ingredients

4 cups flour

2 cups amasi/inkomazi

1 cup sugar

250g butter

1 cup fresh milk

3 eggs

4 tsp baking powder

1 tbsp vanilla essence

Instructions

  1. Mix all dry ingredients together in a bowl.
  2. Add butter to the dry ingredients and mix.
  3. Mix wet ingredients in a jar.
  4. Combine the dry and wet ingredients together. The mixture should not be runny.
  5. Place the batter in the fridge for an hour, or for 30 mins if you're in a hurry.
  6. Sprinkle flour on a clean surface, roll out the batter, and cut your desired shapes.
  7. Glaze the scones with the remaining wet ingredients.
  8. Bake according to your oven temperature.

Fudgy Brownies

Ingredients

10 tablespoons (145g) unsalted butter

1 ¼ cups (250g) granulated sugar

3/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons cocoa powder,

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt

2 large cold eggs

2/3 cup (80g) chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Grease shallow pan with butter
  2. Line with baking sheet
  3. Melt butter and chocolate on the stove.
  4. While mixture is still hoot, add sugar,eggs and whisk until creamy.
  5. Add salt,vanilla essence ,flour, cocoa and fold gently
  6. Pour batter into baking tray.
  7. Skake and top gently...bake
  8. Cool completely before removing the brownies from the pan (this step is essential and helps the brownies set). Cut into 16 squares

Ginger biscuit

Ingredients

3 cups cake flour

1 ½ cups sugar

125g butter

1 ½ teaspoon fine gingert

½ pkt creat tartar

½ pkt bicarbonated soda

2 eggs

125 g golgen syrup

Instructions

  1. Melt butter, sugar and syrup slowly on a medium heat.
  2. Stir occassionally until butter has melted & let it cool.
  3. sift flour,ginger,biocarb, cream of tartar and add cooled butter mixture.
  4. Add egg one at a time and mix after each addition.
  5. Make small balls and place them in a baking tray
  6. Bake until buiscuit has platten and browned.

Jam squares

Ingredients

¼ cups of sugar

1 egg

¾ cups of jams

Instructions

  2. Add flour,baking powder and mix to form dough. use your hands to press dough together.
  3. Use ⅔ of the dough & spread along the base of greased pan
  4. Use fork to poke the holes into the dough & lather the Jam nice and thick
  5. grate the remaining dough on the dough & scatter on top of jam.
  6. If dough is soft put it in the fridge
  7. Bake
